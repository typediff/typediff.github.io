Baskerville vs Times New Roman

Sage CEGJQ

Sage CEGJQ

  1. Time's serifs are sharp and long.
  2. Baskerville has a ball on the ‘g’ and an open loop.
  3. Baskerville is one of the few typefaces with a serif on the bottom of it's ‘C’.
  4. Baskerville's bottom horizontal stroke is much longer than the upper strokes, giving it a kind of underbite.
  5. The vertical stroke in Baskerville's ‘G’ is relatively short.
  6. The vertical stroke in Times's ‘G’ is unusually long.
  7. Baskerville's ‘J’ descends below the baseline.
  8. Baskerville has a very decorative, swashy tail on its ‘Q’
